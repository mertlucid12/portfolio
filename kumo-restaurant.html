Our Philosophy

The art of impermanence

KUMO — meaning "cloud" in Japanese — was born from a singular obsession: the fleeting perfection of a single, extraordinary bite. Every dish is a meditation on impermanence, crafted to exist only in this moment.

We source exclusively from a curated network of Japanese farmers, coastal fishermen, and artisan producers. The menu shifts with the seasons, honouring the ancient philosophy of shun — the brief window when an ingredient reaches its absolute peak.

The Setting

A world unto itself

Step through our doors and the city dissolves. KUMO's interior is a study in refined restraint — hand-plastered walls, aged hinoki wood, and the soft percussion of a bamboo water feature.

The centerpiece is our 18-seat omakase counter, where you dine in direct dialogue with the chef. Every gesture, every placement, is considered. This is theatre as hospitality.

The Craftsmen

Meet the chefs

K
Kenji Murakami
Executive Chef & Owner

Trained under Jiro Ono for seven years before cutting his own path. Kenji brings a lifetime of mastery to every plate — technique is invisible, only flavour remains.

A
Aiko Tanaka
Head Sushi Chef

One of only a handful of women to hold the title of shokunin in Japan, Aiko's nigiri is architectural: precise, balanced, and alive with umami.

R
Riku Hayashi
Pastry & Wagashi Chef

Riku studied traditional wagashi in Kyoto for five years before fusing those techniques with French patisserie. His desserts close the meal like the final note of a haiku.
